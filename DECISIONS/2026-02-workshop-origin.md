# Workshop Origin

**Date:** February 2026
**Decision:** Establish a dedicated workshop for offline-first creative tools

## Why this exists

Most creative software has moved toward cloud dependency, subscription models, and continuous partial attention. Features appear and disappear. Data lives on remote servers. Tools become services.

This workshop exists because that model does not serve focused creative work.

## The shift

The decision to build offline-first, owned tools comes from direct experience with:

- Software that stops working when companies change business models
- Subscription fatigue and the erosion of ownership
- Cloud services that introduce latency, complexity, and privacy concerns
- Feature bloat driven by growth metrics rather than user needs
- Tools that demand constant updates and break existing workflows

These are not hypothetical problems. They are real friction in daily creative practice.

## The alternative

This workshop builds tools that:

- Work without internet access
- Belong to the user after purchase
- Store data locally in readable formats
- Remain stable over years
- Respect focus and craft

This is not a rejection of all modern software practices. It is a deliberate choice to prioritize stability, ownership, and trust over scale and recurring revenue.

## Projects

Initial projects:

- **ColorWizard Desktop** — A painting and color literacy tool
- **Magpie** — An embroidery and pattern translation tool

Both are scoped as complete v1 products, not platforms for future expansion.

## Constraints

These constraints are permanent:

- No cloud dependency
- No subscription models
- No user accounts
- Offline-first operation
- Local, readable data storage
- Stable, proven technology choices

## What success looks like

Success is not measured in user growth or revenue scale. Success is building tools that:

- Work reliably in five or ten years
- Solve specific problems completely
- Respect the people who use them
- Require no ongoing relationship with their creator

This is a workshop for building tools worth keeping.
